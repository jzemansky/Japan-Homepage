Kyoto Plans

Inari Shrine

The Inari Shrine if famous for having a pathway made up of over a thousand torii gates. These torii gates represent the transition from the mundane to the sacred within Shinto, the original religion of Japan.

Imperial Palace

The Imperial Palace used to be the Capital seat of Japan. The emperor would live here and rule of all of their controlled territories.

Gion Odori

The Gion Odori Festival is held in the beginning of November, and is one of the few ways that foreigners can interact and witness the Geisha and Maiko. They perform several rituals and ceremonies within the historic district of Gion in Kyoto.

Fukujuen Uji

Fukujuen Uji is a tea house located alongside the bank of the Uji river. They offer the ability to grind and prepare matcha tea through traditional stone grinding techniques.
